Approach

How I work

Relational at the core

Change happens within relationship. The therapeutic connection isn't just context -- it's an active part of how transformation occurs. What happens between us matters.

Trauma-informed depth

Drawing on extensive training in AEDP and CSRT, I work with how early experiences live in your nervous system and relational patterns -- not just your conscious narrative.

Pattern-focused, not symptom-focused

Rather than managing symptoms, we address the underlying patterns that create them. This means working with roots, not just branches.

Emotionally engaged

Insight alone rarely produces lasting change. We integrate cognitive understanding with emotional and embodied experience -- thinking, feeling, and sensing together.

Philosophy

What I believe about change

Change happens through relationship

The therapeutic relationship isn't a backdrop -- it's the primary vehicle for transformation. What happens between us creates the conditions for real internal reorganization.

Insight alone is not enough

Understanding why you feel what you feel rarely changes the feeling. Change requires working at the level of emotion, body, and relational pattern.

Patterns live in the nervous system

Our earliest experiences shape how we relate, respond, and make meaning. These templates operate below conscious awareness and require more than insight to shift.

Therapy should be active and engaged

I don't work from behind a neutral stance. Real therapeutic work requires presence, attunement, and a willingness to meet you where you are.

Questions

Frequently asked

Core Self Reclamation Therapy (CSRT) is an experiential and accelerated therapeutic model that goes beyond traditional talk therapy. Rather than spending months or years building toward insight, CSRT works directly with emotional and relational patterns at a deep, felt level -- creating lasting change in a focused timeframe.

The program is designed for professionals, creatives, and caregivers -- high-functioning adults who understand themselves intellectually but feel stuck in recurring emotional or relationship patterns. If you're ready to do real work, not just talk about it, this may be a good fit.

Many clients find the 10-session arc provides what they need. For those who want continued support, ongoing therapy, follow-up tune-up sessions, and crisis intervention are all available. The structure flexes to meet you where you are.

I am an out-of-network provider. I can provide a superbill for you to submit to your insurance for potential reimbursement. Many PPO plans offer partial coverage for out-of-network therapy.

I do not offer couples counseling or substance abuse treatment. However, relationship dynamics are a central part of the individual work I do -- we absolutely work with how you show up in relationships and what patterns are driving difficulty there.

Periwink is a separate platform I founded to support women navigating midlife and hormonal transitions at a broader level -- through community, education, and whole-person care. My clinical practice and Periwink are complementary but distinct. You can learn more at yourperiwink.com.

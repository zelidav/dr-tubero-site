Approach

How I work

Relational at the core

Change happens within relationship. The therapeutic connection isn't just context—it's an active part of how transformation occurs. What happens between us matters.

Trauma-informed depth

Drawing on extensive training in AEDP and CSRT, I work with how early experiences live in your nervous system and relational patterns—not just your conscious narrative.

Pattern-focused, not symptom-focused

Rather than managing symptoms, we address the underlying patterns that create them. This means working with roots, not just branches.

Emotionally engaged

Insight alone rarely produces lasting change. We integrate cognitive understanding with emotional and embodied experience—thinking, feeling, and sensing together.

About

Dr. Adrian Tubero

I work with people who sense that something deeper is driving their struggles—patterns in relationships, emotional responses that feel disproportionate to circumstances, or a persistent feeling of being stuck despite years of reflection.

Over more than twenty years in private practice, I've come to understand psychological change as something that unfolds within relationship, emotional safety, and deep internal reorganization. Insight matters, but it's rarely sufficient. The patterns we carry live in our nervous systems and relational templates—they require more than understanding to shift.

My training reflects this orientation. I've studied extensively in Accelerated Experiential Dynamic Psychotherapy (AEDP) and Core Self Reclamation Therapy (CSRT), an emerging model developed by SueAnne Piliero, Ph.D. I trained directly under Dr. Piliero for over three years and now serve as a supervisor and experiential assistant in her teaching work. This depth shapes everything about how I practice.

Working with me tends to feel different from other therapy experiences—more relationally engaged, more emotionally present. I don't work from behind a neutral stance. I meet you in the work. That's where change actually happens.

Philosophy

What I believe about change

Change happens through relationship

The therapeutic relationship isn't a backdrop—it's the primary vehicle for transformation. What happens between us creates the conditions for internal reorganization.

Insight alone is not enough

Understanding why you feel what you feel rarely changes the feeling. Change requires working at the level of emotion, body, and relational pattern.

Patterns live in the nervous system

Our earliest experiences shape how we relate, respond, and make meaning. These templates operate below conscious awareness and require more than insight to shift.

Therapy is active and engaged

I don't work from behind a neutral stance. Real therapeutic work requires presence, attunement, and a willingness to meet you where you are.
